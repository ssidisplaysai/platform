# Evolution Model

Status: Approved
Classification: Genesis Standard
Type: Living System Model

## Purpose

Define how Genesis evolves as a living knowledge system through governed feedback between reality, research, standards, compilation, and execution.

## Evolution Cycle

Discovery

-> Research

-> Experiment

-> Evidence

-> Peer Review

-> Specification

-> Compiler

-> Runtime

-> Execution

-> Observation

-> Continuous Improvement

## Stage Intent

Discovery:
- identify potentially meaningful patterns or anomalies

Research:
- frame questions and hypotheses with method constraints

Experiment:
- test hypotheses under reproducible conditions

Evidence:
- capture traceable support or refutation signals

Peer Review:
- independently evaluate method, evidence, and interpretation

Specification:
- formalize approved findings as standards candidates

Compiler:
- operationalize specifications through deterministic transformation logic

Runtime:
- execute projected systems in operational context

Execution:
- produce outcomes, traces, and measurable effects

Observation:
- capture new signals from execution and enterprise reality

Continuous Improvement:
- incorporate validated learning into future research and specification cycles

## Why Genesis Is a Living Knowledge System

Genesis treats architecture and software as evolving projections of validated understanding.
Because enterprise reality changes, Genesis must continuously re-observe, re-test, and re-validate.
The system is living because learning is structural, governed, and recursive rather than ad hoc.

## Evolution Constraints

- every loop must preserve evidence lineage
- no canonical promotion without verification and review
- standards and implementation must remain aligned through governance
- speed of iteration must not bypass trust controls
