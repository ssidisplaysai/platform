## 1. Document Identity

GCSA-0005 defines the constitutional identity framework for Genesis and establishes identity as a first-class architectural concept governed above implementation concerns.

## 2. Purpose

This framework defines constitutional identity doctrine, constraints, governance relationships, lifecycle expectations, and conformance obligations that all present and future Genesis architectural domains shall honor.

## 3. Scope

This framework governs constitutional identity only. It does not define authentication, authorization, identity providers, IAM architecture, runtime identity services, APIs, compiler implementation, persistence models, infrastructure models, deployment models, programming languages, frameworks, cloud providers, or vendor technologies.

## 4. Constitutional Authority

This framework derives authority from the Genesis Constitution and conforms to GAV-0001, GAF-0001, ABL-0001 Version 1.0.1, GCSA-PROGRAM-0001, and GCSA-0004.

## 5. Identity Philosophy

Identity is a constitutional concern because identity defines enduring subject continuity, authority traceability, and governance legitimacy across architectural evolution. Implementation mechanisms may realize identity, but they shall not define constitutional identity meaning.

## 6. Identity Objectives

The constitutional objectives of identity are:

- preserve enduring subject continuity
- ensure governance lineage traceability
- maintain architectural dependency clarity
- enable constitutional lifecycle continuity
- constrain architectural evolution without semantic drift
- remain implementation independent and technology neutral

## 7. Identity Principles

Constitutional identity principles are:

- identity shall be stable and non-ambiguous
- identity shall be authority-bound and governance-traceable
- identity shall be namespace-constrained and non-colliding
- identity shall be lifecycle-resilient
- identity shall be independent from storage and implementation location
- identity shall be preserved across publication and evolution

## 8. Constitutional Laws of Identity

The following laws are immutable constitutional law:

- every constitutional object possesses one constitutional identity
- identity is immutable
- identity survives lifecycle changes
- identity is implementation-independent
- identity cannot be reassigned
- identity cannot be reused
- identity is globally unique within its constitutional namespace
- versions evolve while identity persists
- identity precedes implementation
- identity participates in governance lineage

## 9. Definition of Identity

Constitutional identity is the enduring, governance-legitimate, non-reassignable architectural designation of a constitutional subject that preserves subject continuity through version, lifecycle, lineage, dependency, and publication transitions.

## 10. Identity Domains

Constitutional identity domains include:

- enterprise
- organization
- domain

- artifact
- evidence
- knowledge
- review

- policy

## 11. Identity Categories

Identity categories include constitutional source identities, governance identities, relationship identities, lineage identities, dependency identities, and publication identities. Categories classify architectural meaning and governance role, not implementation form.

## 12. Identity Types

Each constitutional domain may define specialized identity types subordinate to this framework. Specialized types shall preserve all constitutional identity laws and shall not redefine constitutional identity meaning.

## 13. Identity Attributes

Constitutional identity attributes include:

- subject continuity

- governance classification context
- lifecycle continuity context
- lineage continuity context
- dependency traceability context
- publication continuity context

## 14. Identity Metadata

Identity metadata shall record governance-relevant context required for constitutional interpretation, lineage traceability, dependency interpretation, lifecycle state understanding, and baseline conformance verification.

## 15. Identity Stability

Identity stability guarantees include:

- Identity exists independently of filenames.
- Identity exists independently of repositories.
- Identity exists independently of folders.
- Identity exists independently of URLs.
- Identity exists independently of storage.
- Identity exists independently of databases.
- Identity exists independently of runtime objects.
- Identity exists independently of source code.
- Identity exists independently of deployments.

## 16. Identity Boundaries

Identity boundaries are constitutional boundaries. Identity shall survive movement, renaming, serialization, publication, compilation, migration, import, export, replication, and runtime execution. Identity shall never be reassigned, duplicated, reused, made dependent on storage location, or made dependent on implementation.

## 17. Parent and Child Identity

Parent-child identity relationships define constitutional containment and derivation order. Parentage constrains subordinate meaning; child identities inherit constitutional constraints without losing distinct identity.

## 18. Ownership

Ownership defines stewardship accountability for identity declarations and continuity. Ownership does not supersede constitutional authority and does not permit identity reassignment.

## 19. Governance

Governance defines legitimacy of identity declaration, lifecycle transitions, dependency standing, and publication standing. Identity legitimacy is constitutional and review-governed.

## 20. Lineage

Lineage defines historical and structural continuity of constitutional subjects across governed evolution. Identity participates in lineage so architectural history remains auditable and non-ambiguous.

## 21. Version Relationships

Identity and version are distinct. Identity designates constitutional subject continuity; version designates governed state of that subject at a point in architectural evolution. Version may change; identity persists.

## 22. Dependency Relationships

Dependency relationships are identity-referenced constitutional relationships that establish directional architectural reliance. Dependencies shall be explicit, traceable, and constitutionally coherent.

## 23. Reference Relationships

Reference relationships link identities for governance interpretation, review traceability, and architectural coherence. Reference linkage shall not redefine identity ownership or constitutional meaning.

## 24. Identity Visibility

Identity visibility classifications govern constitutional disclosure boundaries such as internal constitutional visibility, governed shared visibility, and external reference visibility. Visibility classification is governance policy, not implementation access control.

## 25. Identity Declaration

Identity declaration is a constitutional act that establishes a governance-legitimate identity for a constitutional subject within its namespace and authority context.

## 26. Identity Evolution

Identity evolves through versioned constitutional refinement without identity replacement. Evolution may clarify scope or standing while preserving subject continuity and governance lineage.

## 27. Identity Persistence

Identity persistence requires continuity across constitutional repositories, publication states, and lifecycle transitions. Persistence is constitutional continuity, not storage-specific persistence behavior.

## 28. Identity Immutability

Immutability prohibits reassignment, substitution, or semantic repurposing of an identity once constitutionally declared.

## 29. Identity Retirement

Retirement marks end-of-active constitutional use while preserving historical legitimacy, lineage continuity, and traceability.

## 30. Identity Archival

Archival preserves historical identity evidence and governance context for long-horizon constitutional auditability.

## 31. Framework Integration

This framework occupies the Identity domain defined within GAF-0001 and integrates with constitutional layer constraints, dependency law, and governed architectural evolution.

## 32. Baseline Conformance

All future constitutional artifacts shall declare baseline conformance and shall identify the baseline to which they conform.

## 33. Constitutional Artifact Integration

This framework integrates with constitutional artifacts by providing common identity doctrine for artifact continuity, review continuity, correction continuity, decision continuity, policy continuity, and baseline continuity.

Future constitutional architectures, including Enterprise Identity, Runtime Identity, Knowledge Identity, Semantic Identity, Security Identity, and External Identity, shall specialize this framework and shall not redefine constitutional identity.

## 34. Enterprise Integration

Enterprise-wide identity consistency shall be preserved through constitutional identity laws, lineage continuity, and governed dependency declarations across enterprise architectural domains.

## 35. Runtime Independence

Runtime identity implementations shall conform to this framework and shall not redefine constitutional identity meaning.

## 36. Technology Neutrality

This framework is technology-independent and implementation-independent. No technology stack, provider, product, or runtime mechanism is constitutive of constitutional identity.

## 37. Governance Requirements

Every future constitutional artifact shall declare:

- framework placement
- baseline conformance
- architectural dependencies
- governance lineage
