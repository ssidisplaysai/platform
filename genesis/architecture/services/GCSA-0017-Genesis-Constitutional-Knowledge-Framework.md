## 1. Artifact Identity

GCSA-0017 defines the Genesis Constitutional Knowledge Framework as a proposed constitutional primitive specification for Knowledge semantics.

## 2. Constitutional Authority

This artifact derives constitutional authority from GAV-0001, GAF-0001, ABL-0001, and GCP-0001 Version 1.0.0 - APPROVED.

## 3. Purpose

Define Knowledge as the constitutional primitive representing governed, attributable, evidence-grounded understanding that preserves meaning, provenance, lineage, confidence, validity, and implementation independence.

Knowledge is not merely stored information.

Knowledge is not raw Evidence.

Knowledge must be grounded in one or more Evidence records.

Knowledge may organize, interpret, synthesize, contextualize, or derive understanding from Evidence.

Knowledge never directs behavior.

Knowledge never performs Action.

Knowledge never changes State.

Knowledge never becomes Decision.

Knowledge never becomes Event.

Knowledge never becomes Command.

Knowledge never becomes Query.

Knowledge never becomes Reply.

Knowledge never becomes Evidence.

Knowledge is a first-class Genesis Constitutional Primitive.

## 4. Scope

In scope are constitutional Knowledge semantics including identity, type, evidence grounding, meaning, subject, context, attribution, provenance, lineage, confidence, validity, temporal context, classification, visibility, authorization context, governance, and conformance.

Out of scope are transport mechanics, storage implementation internals, runtime execution behavior, and API protocol specifics.

## 5. Primitive Declaration

Knowledge is a constitutional primitive candidate under GCP-0001 and is designated CANDIDATE FOR APPROVAL pending independent review.

An AI output alone is not constitutional Knowledge.

## 40. Constitutional Laws

Law 1. Constitutional Law of Knowledge Identity
- Law Statement: Every Knowledge record shall have independent constitutional identity.
- Constitutional Meaning: identity persists across lifecycle and representation changes.
- Required Guarantees: uniqueness, continuity, lineage preservation.
- Prohibited Conditions: identity conflation, silent replacement.
- Conformance Test: verify identity stability through lifecycle transitions.

Law 2. Constitutional Law of Knowledge Type
- Law Statement: Every Knowledge record shall declare explicit Knowledge Type.
- Constitutional Meaning: type governs knowledge semantics without behavior implication.
- Required Guarantees: explicit type and deterministic interpretation.
- Prohibited Conditions: hidden type inference from implementation details.
- Conformance Test: verify explicit type presence and stable interpretation.

- Constitutional Meaning: Evidence grounds are necessary for constitutional existence.
