### PART I - FOUNDATION

## 1. Document Identity

GCSA-0008 defines Policy as a Genesis Constitutional Primitive specification under GCP-0001 governance and aligned with GPD-0001 discovery authority.

## 2. Purpose

This framework defines what policies are, how they derive authority, what they govern, how they apply, and how they evolve within constitutional boundaries.

## 3. Constitutional Scope

In scope are Policy Identity, Policy Authority, Policy Scope, subjects, objects, applicability, conditions, obligations, permissions, prohibitions, exceptions, validity, Policy State, versioning, supersession, conflict resolution, governance, and evolution.

Out of scope are authorization, access control, runtime evaluation, workflow, implementation logic, event processing, decisions, and lifecycle execution.

## 4. Constitutional Authority

This specification is subordinate to the Genesis Constitution and governed through GAV-0001, GAF-0001, ABL-0001 Version 1.0.1, and GCP-0001 Version 1.0.0 — APPROVED.

## 6. Primitive Classification

Policy is specified as a Constitutional Primitive candidate under GCP-0001 and SHALL be interpreted as CANDIDATE FOR APPROVAL until independently reviewed.

## 7. Constitutional Question

How does Genesis constitutionally define governed normative constraints and allowances without collapsing into authorization, execution, or implementation behavior?

## 8. Owned Concept

Policy owns the constitutional concept of declarative governance rules that constrain and guide constitutional behavior without executing runtime control.

## 9. Explicit Exclusions

Policy SHALL NOT own:

- authorization
- access control execution
- permission enforcement
- runtime evaluation logic
- workflow orchestration
- implementation logic
- event processing
- decision execution
- lifecycle execution

## 10. Framework Objectives

Objectives:

- establish Policy Identity and authority derivation
- define policy scope, subjects, objects, and applicability
- govern conditions, obligations, permissions, prohibitions, and exceptions
- govern validity, Policy State, versioning, supersession, and conflict resolution
- ensure constitutional governance, auditability, and implementation independence

## 11. Framework Non-Objectives

Non-objectives:

- runtime execution semantics
- system-specific policy engines
- deployment-specific control planes
- workflow state-machine design
- event processor implementation

## 12. Constitutional Policy Definition

Policy is a constitutionally governed, declarative normative construct that establishes what is allowed, required, prohibited, and excepted for defined subjects and objects within explicit scope and validity boundaries.

### PART II - CONSTITUTIONAL LAWS

## 13. Constitutional Laws

Law 1 — Independent Policy Identity  
Every Policy SHALL possess its own independent constitutional Identity.

Law 2 — Derived Constitutional Authority  
Every Policy SHALL derive authority from explicit constitutional governance lineage.

Law 3 — Governance Without Execution  
Policies SHALL govern behavior but SHALL NOT execute behavior.

Law 4 — Constraint Without Decision Execution  
Policies SHALL constrain behavior but SHALL NOT perform decision execution.

Law 5 — Explicit Applicability  
Policy applicability SHALL explicitly identify applicable scope, subjects, and objects.

Law 6 — Explicit Obligations  
Policy obligations SHALL be explicit, testable, and auditable.

Law 7 — Explicit Prohibitions  
Policy prohibitions SHALL be explicit, testable, and auditable.

Law 8 — Declarative Permissions  
Policy permissions SHALL be declarative statements and SHALL NOT imply enforcement mechanics.

Law 9 — Authorization Boundary  
Authorization is outside Policy ownership and SHALL remain separately governed.

Law 10 — Deterministic Conflict Resolution  
Policy conflict resolution SHALL be deterministic and constitutionally governed.

Law 11 — Supersession Lineage Preservation  
Policy supersession SHALL preserve provenance and lineage of predecessor policies.

Law 12 — Implementation Independence  
Policies SHALL remain implementation-independent and technology-neutral.

Law 13 — Constitutional Governance Integrity  
Policy governance SHALL remain constitutionally bounded and evidence-based.

Law 14 — Semantic Immutability Without Constitutional Revision  
Policy foundational semantics SHALL NOT be changed without constitutional revision.

Law 15 — Auditability Preservation  
Policies SHALL preserve auditable identity, authority, applicability, and change lineage.

Law 16 — Acyclic Constitutional Dependency  
No Policy may introduce constitutional dependency cycles.

## 14. Policy Identity

Policy Identity SHALL uniquely identify the policy construct independently of any subject, object, relationship, state instance, runtime engine, or implementation detail.

## 15. Policy Authority

Policy authority SHALL identify constitutional source, governance authority, and approval lineage required for valid policy admission.

## 16. Policy Scope

Policy scope SHALL define constitutional boundaries in which policy semantics are meaningful and enforceable as declarations.

## 17. Policy Subjects

Policy subjects SHALL identify the governed constitutional participants or constructs to which policy declarations apply.

## 18. Policy Objects

Policy objects SHALL identify the constitutional resources, relations, states, or contexts that policy declarations govern.

## 19. Applicability

Applicability SHALL be explicit and SHALL define when, where, and to whom policy declarations apply.

## 20. Conditions

Policy conditions SHALL be declarative predicates defining qualifying circumstances without prescribing evaluation implementation.

## 21. Obligations

Obligations SHALL define required constitutional behaviors or states under explicit applicability and validity bounds.

## 22. Permissions

Permissions SHALL be declarative policy statements and SHALL NOT be interpreted as authorization engines or enforcement instructions.

## 23. Prohibitions

Prohibitions SHALL define disallowed constitutional behaviors or states with explicit scope, subjects, objects, and conditions.

## 24. Exceptions

Exceptions SHALL be explicit bounded overrides that remain subordinate to constitutional authority and auditable lineage.

## 25. Validity

Validity SHALL govern when policy declarations are constitutionally active, inactive, suspended, expired, superseded, or invalid.

## 26. Policy State

Policy State SHALL represent policy condition and lifecycle-like status indicators while remaining distinct from Policy semantic identity and ownership.

## 27. Versioning and Supersession

Versioning SHALL track policy revisions; supersession SHALL establish successor-predecessor lineage without erasing constitutional history.

## 28. Conflict Resolution

Conflict resolution SHALL define deterministic precedence rules, tie-break rules, exception boundaries, and constitutional escalation requirements.

### PART III - BOUNDARIES AND DISTINCTIONS

## 29. Policy Versus Relationship

Policy is distinct from Relationship. Relationship defines governed connection semantics among participants; Policy defines declarative governance constraints and allowances that may contextually reference relationships.

## 30. Policy Versus Identity

Policy is distinct from Identity. Identity establishes constitutional uniqueness; Policy governs declarations associated with subjects, objects, and applicability.

## 31. Policy Versus State

Policy is distinct from State. State represents condition; Policy represents declarative normative governance.

## 32. Policy Versus Authorization and Permission Enforcement

Policy is distinct from authorization and permission enforcement. Policy declarations SHALL NOT be interpreted as execution logic for access decisions or grants.

## 33. Policy Versus Decision and Workflow

Policy is distinct from Decision and Workflow. Policy constrains and guides but does not execute decisions or orchestrate workflow transitions.

## 34. Policy Versus Runtime Configuration and Implementation

Policy is distinct from runtime configuration and implementation logic. No runtime engine detail may redefine constitutional policy meaning.

## 35. Policy Versus Event and Lifecycle

Policy is distinct from Event and Lifecycle execution. Policy may contextually reference event or lifecycle contexts without owning event processing or lifecycle execution semantics.

### PART IV - DEPENDENCY, GOVERNANCE, AND CONFORMANCE

## 36. Dependency Model and Acyclicity

Approved foundational dependency:

- Policy -> Identity

Contextual references only:

- Policy -> State (contextual only)
- Policy -> Relationship (contextual only)

State is contextual only.

Relationship is contextual only.

Neither State nor Relationship may become required ownership dependencies.

No Genesis Constitutional Primitive may introduce a circular dependency.

Explicit acyclicity declarations:

- no direct cycle exists
- no indirect cycle exists
- no hidden semantic cycle exists
- no specialization cycle exists
- no governance cycle exists

## 37. Governance and Evolution

Policy governance SHALL require constitutional authority, explicit rationale, evidence, provenance, and lineage for creation, modification, supersession, and retirement.

Policy semantics are immutable without constitutional revision.
