## 1. Artifact Identity

GCSA-0016 defines the Genesis Constitutional Evidence Framework as a proposed constitutional primitive specification for Evidence semantics.

## 2. Constitutional Authority

This artifact derives constitutional authority from GAV-0001, GAF-0001, ABL-0001, and GCP-0001 Version 1.0.0 - APPROVED.

## 3. Purpose

Define Evidence as the constitutional primitive representing governed, immutable, attributable information supporting constitutional knowledge, reasoning, auditability, traceability, and deterministic decision making.

Evidence never directs behavior.

Evidence never performs Action.

Evidence never changes State.

Evidence never becomes Event.

Evidence never becomes Decision.

Evidence never becomes Query.

Evidence never becomes Reply.

Evidence never becomes Command.

Evidence is immutable once constitutionally established.

Evidence is a first-class Genesis Constitutional Primitive.

## 4. Scope

In scope are constitutional Evidence semantics including identity, type, origin, source, subject, attribution, provenance, lineage, authenticity, integrity, temporal context, classification, visibility, authorization context, confidence, validation status, governance, and conformance.

Out of scope are transport mechanics, storage implementation internals, runtime execution behavior, and API protocol specifics.

## 5. Primitive Declaration

Evidence is a constitutional primitive candidate under GCP-0001 and is designated CANDIDATE FOR APPROVAL pending independent review.

## 6. Evidence Definition

A governed constitutional record of observed, derived, measured, asserted, or otherwise attributable information that preserves identity, provenance, lineage, attribution, temporal context, authenticity, immutability, and implementation independence without directing behavior or mutating constitutional state.

The definition establishes:

- stable identity
- explicit type
- origin
- source
- subject
- attribution
- provenance
- lineage
- authenticity
- integrity
- temporal context
- classification
- visibility
- authorization context
- confidence
- validation status
- immutability
- implementation independence

## 7. Evidence Constitutional Necessity

Evidence is constitutionally necessary as the primitive for attributable information truth claims that may support reasoning and decisions without becoming behavior or directive semantics.

## 15. Evidence Type

Evidence Type identifies governed evidence category semantics and does not imply behavior direction.

## 16. Origin and Source

Origin identifies how evidence came into constitutional existence.

Source identifies where or from whom evidence was obtained.

Origin and source are explicit, attributable, and lineage-preserving.

## 17. Subject and Attribution

Subject identifies what evidence is about.

Attribution identifies accountable originator, collector, generator, or asserting party under governance controls.

## 18. Observed Information and Classification

Observed information captures attributable content.

Classification expresses evidence category and sensitivity semantics without mutating referenced primitive ownership.

## 19. Confidence and Validation Status

Confidence expresses explicit confidence semantics.

Validation Status expresses evidentiary validation state, including unresolved or indeterminate outcomes when required.

## 20. Provenance

Provenance captures evidence acquisition, transformation, custodianship, and origin context in a traceable manner.

## 21. Lineage

Lineage preserves derivation and relationship history across amendments, redactions, transformations, and references while preserving identity continuity.

## 22. Authenticity

Authenticity semantics govern verifiable authorship, origin legitimacy, and tamper-resistance assertions.

## 23. Integrity

Integrity semantics govern completeness, non-corruption, and verifiability of evidence content and associated metadata.

## 24. Authorization Context

Authorization context constrains governed access and utilization of evidence and remains distinct from evidence ownership.

## 25. Visibility

Visibility semantics define disclosure scope, redaction boundaries, and audience constraints without changing evidence identity.

## 26. Temporal Context

Temporal context includes creation, observation, measurement, ingestion, validation, expiration, suspension, and retention timing semantics.

## 27. Correlation and Causation References

Correlation links related evidence and contextual artifacts without implying causation.

Causation references may be explicit and remain non-foundational.

## 28. Lifecycle

Lifecycle semantics may include Draft, Collected, Validated, Disputed, Redacted, Superseded, Retained, Archived, and Expired classifications.

Lifecycle transitions preserve identity and lineage.

## 29. Retention

Retention semantics define constitutional evidence preservation obligations and disposition conditions while preserving lineage traceability.

## 30. Versioning and Compatibility

Versioning preserves identity continuity and semantic traceability.

Compatibility preserves interpretive continuity across representation evolution.

## 31. Composition and Specialization

Composition supports aggregate evidence structures while preserving constituent identity where required.

Specialization refines evidence categories without altering foundational dependency or ownership boundaries.

## 32. Governance and Evolution

Governance covers evidence policy alignment, lifecycle controls, certification controls, and reviewability.

Evolution must preserve ownership boundaries, dependency direction, reverse exclusions, immutability guarantees, and implementation independence.

## 33. Conformance

Conformance requires deterministic interpretation of evidence semantics independent of runtime, transport, storage, or API implementation.

## 34. Distinction from Identity and State

Identity is foundational continuity; State is condition.

Evidence may describe either contextually while owning neither and mutating neither.

## 35. Distinction from Relationship and Policy

Relationship is governed association; Policy defines enduring governance rules.

Evidence may reference these contextually while preserving their ownership boundaries.

## 36. Distinction from Capability and Action

Capability is potential ability and Action performs behavior.

Evidence may describe or support claims about these primitives but never becomes behavior.

## 37. Distinction from Decision and Event

Decision is governed determination and Event asserts occurrence.

Evidence may support decisions and describe events, but Evidence never becomes Decision or Event.

## 38. Distinction from Command, Query, and Reply

Command directs behavior, Query requests information, and Reply answers information.

Evidence may inform any of these but never becomes Command, Query, or Reply.

## 39. Distinction from Workflow, Runtime, and Implementation

Workflow coordinates progression, Runtime executes operations, and Implementation realizes technology.

Evidence remains constitutionally distinct and implementation-independent.
