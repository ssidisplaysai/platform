# Architectural Layers

Status: Approved
Classification: Genesis Standard
Type: Layer Model

## Layer 1: Reality

Purpose:
- represent the enterprise world as it exists independent of software

Inputs:
- real-world operations, actors, events, constraints, and outcomes

Outputs:
- observable phenomena

Responsibilities:
- provide the source domain for all upstream modeling

Dependencies:
- none

Future evolution:
- broader observation channels and richer enterprise contexts

## Layer 2: Research

Purpose:
- investigate observations through hypotheses, experiments, and peer review

Inputs:
- observations from reality and prior findings

Outputs:
- evidence-backed discoveries, limitations, and candidate insights

Responsibilities:
- test claims before standards promotion

Dependencies:
- Reality

Future evolution:
- stronger experiment libraries and repeatability tooling

## Layer 3: Standards

Purpose:
- define canonical doctrine, semantic rules, and governance constraints

Inputs:
- validated research discoveries and constitutional constraints

Outputs:
- approved specifications and canonical definitions

Responsibilities:
- stabilize meaning and constraints independent of implementation

Dependencies:
- Research, Constitution

Future evolution:
- controlled extension models across industries and organizations

## Layer 4: Compiler

Purpose:
- transform evidence-grounded inputs into validated intermediate representations deterministically

Inputs:
- source inputs plus governing standards

Outputs:
- auditable IR artifacts and validation outcomes

Responsibilities:
- enforce deterministic transformation and lineage preservation

Dependencies:
- Standards, Evidence, Knowledge structures

Future evolution:
- expanded stage proofs and semantic validation depth

## Layer 5: Enterprise Genome

Purpose:
- maintain a structured canonical enterprise representation with historical continuity

Inputs:
- validated compiler outputs aligned to standards

Outputs:
- enterprise genome state and evolution history

Responsibilities:
- preserve enterprise identity, capability structure, policy lineage, and relational integrity

Dependencies:
- Compiler, Standards

Future evolution:
- advanced continuity and conflict-resolution models

## Layer 6: Blueprint

Purpose:
- project genome state into implementation-oriented design artifacts

Inputs:
- enterprise genome state and projection constraints

Outputs:
- blueprint definitions for applications and systems

Responsibilities:
- maintain projection traceability to upstream canonical sources

Dependencies:
- Enterprise Genome, Standards

Future evolution:
- richer projection profiles for multi-environment deployment

## Layer 7: Runtime

Purpose:
- execute projected applications and system behaviors in operational contexts

Inputs:
- blueprint-derived systems and live operational interactions

Outputs:
- execution results, operational events, and traces

Responsibilities:
- run systems within constraints set by upstream standards and architecture

Dependencies:
- Blueprint, operational infrastructure

Future evolution:
- improved observability and adaptive runtime governance controls

## Layer 8: Living Enterprise

Purpose:
- close the loop from execution back to observation and learning

Inputs:
- runtime outcomes and newly generated evidence

Outputs:
- new observations and research triggers

Responsibilities:
- ensure enterprise learning remains continuous, auditable, and governed

Dependencies:
- Runtime, Research, Standards

Future evolution:
- faster validated learning cycles without sacrificing governance rigor
