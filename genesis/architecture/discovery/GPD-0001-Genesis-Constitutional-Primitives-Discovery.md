## 9. Candidate Definition of a Constitutional Primitive

Finding: a Genesis Constitutional Primitive is a foundational constitutional concept with cross-domain interpretive relevance that cannot be reduced to lower-level primitives without loss of core meaning.

Assessment notes:

- primitive meaning is architectural and constitutional, not implementation behavior
- primitive meaning anchors downstream specifications and service architectures
- primitive concepts require independent governability and independent reviewability

## 10. Primitive Necessity

Assessment: Constitutional Primitives are a distinct layer because they prevent semantic drift between authority and implementation.

Distinctions:

- Primitive vs Specification: a primitive defines foundational concept ownership; a specification defines structured rules within or across concepts.
- Primitive vs Service: a primitive is constitutional meaning; a service is a realizational architecture construct.
- Primitive vs Domain: a primitive is concept-atomic; a domain is a broader responsibility partition.
- Primitive vs Runtime Capability: a primitive is constitutional interpretation; runtime capability is operational behavior.
- Primitive vs Implementation: a primitive is technology-neutral concept law; implementation is realization technique.

## 11. Primitive Admission Criteria

Recommended criteria:

- exactly one foundational concept
- cross-domain applicability
- implementation independence
- semantic irreducibility
- constitutional significance
- independent reviewability
- independent governability
- stable architectural boundary
- non-derivability from lower-level Primitives

## 12. Primitive Rejection Criteria

A candidate is recommended for rejection when one or more of the following appears in evidence:

- meaning is derivative of an existing primitive
- boundary ownership cannot be isolated
- concept is implementation-coupled
- concept is service-level or workflow-level rather than foundational
- governance evidence cannot be made independently reviewable
- overlap risk cannot be controlled without semantic redefinition

## 13. Primitive Inflation Risk

Finding: inflation risk is high when concept granularity is driven by convenience rather than atomic meaning.

Architectural implication:

- too many primitives create dependency congestion and ambiguous ownership
- inflated primitives increase conflict adjudication overhead
- inflation undermines baseline stability

Recommendation:

- restrict admission to concepts that pass every required admission criterion
- prefer specification-level codification for non-atomic concerns

## 14. Primitive Atomicity

Assessment: in this context, atomic means concept ownership that cannot be split without producing semantic contradiction or governance ambiguity.

Proposed atomicity test:

- remove the concept from architecture
- attempt to reconstruct meaning from remaining primitives
- if reconstruction causes ambiguity, the concept remains atomic

## 26. Primitive Specialization Model

Assessment: specialization means narrowing or contextualizing a primitive for a subordinate scope while preserving parent primitive meaning.

Primitive dependency vs Primitive specialization:

- dependency: one primitive references or relies on another
- specialization: one primitive branch refines a primitive's scope without altering core meaning

Primitive specialization vs Primitive redefinition:

- specialization preserves core meaning
- redefinition alters core meaning and is assessed as governance defect

## 27. Primitive Extension Model

Assessment: extension adds non-conflicting attributes or usage constraints around a primitive; it does not alter primitive ownership or core semantics.

Distinction:

- extension adds context
- specialization narrows applicability
- dependency links concepts

## 28. Primitive Dependency Model

Recommended dependency model: directed, explicit, evidence-backed primitive dependencies with no reciprocal loops.

Architectural implication: dependency declarations should be reviewed as first-class governance evidence in future primitive reviews.

## 29. Acyclicity Requirement

Assessment: acyclicity is necessary to preserve constitutional interpretive determinism.

Proposed rule:

No Genesis Constitutional Primitive may introduce a circular dependency.

## 31. Primitive Interaction Model

Interaction assessment: primitives cooperate through explicit references and governed dependency declarations while retaining independent semantic ownership.

Interaction guardrails:

- no primitive absorbs another primitive's ownership
- no primitive interaction implies semantic merge
- cross-primitive interpretation requires lineage and dependency evidence

## 32. Primitive Conflict and Overlap Resolution

Recommended adjudication sequence:

1. identify overlap claim and impacted primitives
2. evaluate ownership definitions and exclusions
3. test against admission and boundary criteria
4. issue correction recommendation or scope clarification
5. re-review affected artifacts under governed process

Architectural implication: overlap resolution should prefer boundary clarification over primitive proliferation.

## 34. Primitive Admission and Evolution Process

Recommended process:

- propose candidate via discovery evidence
- author candidate framework
- complete independent review and corrections when needed
- promote to approved/frozen/published state when evidence supports
- permit supersession with preserved lineage when major semantic evolution is justified
- reject candidate when admission criteria cannot be met

Evolution recommendation: primitive-set growth should be incremental, evidence-led, and bounded by acyclicity and non-redefinition rules.

## 35. Findings and Recommendations

Confirmed Findings:

- Identity and State are confirmed primitives supported by approved frameworks and approved independent reviews.
- Primitive layer distinction from specification, service, domain, runtime capability, and implementation is architecturally necessary.
- Acyclic dependency governance is needed for deterministic primitive interpretation.

Candidate Findings:

- Relationship, Policy, Decision, Event, Lifecycle, and Dependency are viable candidate primitives pending dedicated framework authoring and review.

Risks:

- primitive inflation if admission criteria are relaxed
- semantic overlap between Relationship and Dependency
- semantic overlap between Policy, Decision, and Event

Open Questions:

- whether Event requires primitive-level dependency on Decision or only domain-level dependency
- whether Policy should be split into policy-definition and policy-application primitives

Recommendations:

- codify strict admission and rejection criteria in GCP-0001
- codify acyclicity and overlap-resolution mechanisms in GCP-0001
- prioritize Relationship and Lifecycle after Identity/State consolidation

Blocking Issues:

- None identified for proceeding to GCP-0001 authoring.
