### PART I - ARCHITECTURAL FOUNDATION

## 1. Document Identity

GCP-0001 defines the proposed constitutional architecture for the Genesis Constitutional Primitives layer. GCP-0001 is not itself a Constitutional Primitive.

## 2. Purpose

This architecture SHALL establish permanent governance rules for identifying, admitting, constraining, relating, evolving, and baseline-integrating Genesis Constitutional Primitives.

## 3. Architectural Scope

GCP-0001 governs Primitive definition, admission, rejection, semantic boundaries, relationship declarations, dependency law, conflict resolution, lifecycle governance, evolution, and baseline integration.

GCP-0001 does not govern source code, APIs, database schemas, runtime services, compiler implementation, state-machine implementation, workflow implementation, security implementation, cloud deployment architecture, vendor products, or programming language decisions.

## 4. Constitutional Authority

This architecture derives authority from the Genesis Constitution and SHALL be interpreted through GAV-0001, GAF-0001, and ABL-0001 Version 1.0.1.

### PART II - CONSTITUTIONAL PRIMITIVE DEFINITION

## 9. Constitutional Primitive Definition

A Genesis Constitutional Primitive is an irreducible, implementation-independent constitutional concept that owns one foundational semantic boundary and applies across Genesis domains.

## 10. Primitive Necessity

The Primitive layer exists to prevent semantic drift between constitutional authority and downstream architectures. Primitive specifications SHALL conform to this architecture.

## 11. Primitive Characteristics

Every admissible Primitive SHALL exhibit:

- semantic irreducibility
- foundational significance
- cross-domain applicability
- implementation independence
- technology neutrality
- stable boundary
- independent governability
- independent reviewability
- composability
- constitutional durability

Constitutional Laws of Primitives:

Law 1 - Singular Concept Ownership  
Every Genesis Constitutional Primitive SHALL own exactly one foundational constitutional concept.

Law 2 - Semantic Irreducibility  
A Primitive SHALL NOT be admitted when its entire meaning can be expressed through existing Primitives without constitutional loss.

Law 3 - Implementation Independence  
A Primitive SHALL exist independently of implementation technology, runtime design, programming language, infrastructure, or vendor.

Law 4 - Stable Boundaries  
Every Primitive SHALL declare and preserve a stable semantic boundary.

Law 5 - Explicit Relationships  
Every dependency, specialization, extension, composition, interaction, and reference SHALL be explicitly declared.

Law 6 - Specialization Without Redefinition  
A Primitive MAY specialize an approved Primitive but SHALL NOT redefine, contradict, replace, or weaken it.

Law 7 - Acyclic Dependencies  
Primitive dependencies SHALL form a directed acyclic graph.

Law 8 - Constitutional Governance  
No Primitive may become approved without completing the required governance lifecycle.

Law 9 - Approval Before Reliance  
A Primitive SHALL NOT constitutionally rely on an unapproved Primitive unless the dependency is explicitly provisional and independently reviewed.

Law 10 - Boundary Conflict Resolution  
Primitive boundary conflicts SHALL be resolved through constitutional authority and independent architectural review.

Law 11 - Historical Integrity  
Approval lineage, review records, prior versions, and superseded artifacts SHALL remain historically preserved.

Law 12 - Baseline Integration  
An approved Primitive becomes part of the governing Genesis architecture only through controlled Architecture Baseline integration.

Law 13 - Compatibility Preservation  
Primitive evolution SHALL preserve constitutional compatibility unless a formally governed breaking revision is explicitly approved.

Law 14 - Primitive Inflation Prohibition  
Genesis SHALL NOT create a new Primitive where an existing Primitive or governed composition is sufficient.

Law 15 - Universal Conformance  
All future Genesis Constitutional Primitive specifications SHALL conform to GCP-0001.

## 12. Primitive Atomicity

A Primitive SHALL represent atomic semantic ownership that cannot be split without constitutional ambiguity or contradiction.

## 13. Primitive Universality

A Primitive SHALL be applicable across multiple Genesis domains and SHALL NOT be admitted if constrained to a single narrow implementation context.

## 14. Primitive Independence

A Primitive SHALL be defined independently of runtime mechanics, implementation language, deployment model, and vendor system.

## 15. Primitive Stability

A Primitive boundary SHALL remain stable across revisions unless a formal constitutional revision or supersession is approved through governance lifecycle controls.

## 16. Primitive Distinctions

Required distinctions:

- Primitive versus Specification: Primitive defines foundational meaning; Specification defines governed structure or rules.
- Primitive versus Service: Primitive is constitutional meaning; Service is realizational architecture.
- Primitive versus Domain: Primitive is concept-atomic; Domain is responsibility scope.
- Primitive versus Runtime Capability: Primitive is constitutional semantics; Runtime capability is operational behavior.
- Primitive versus Implementation: Primitive is technology-neutral law; Implementation is realization technique.
- Primitive versus Artifact: Primitive is governed concept; Artifact is a document or evidence container.
- Primitive Architecture versus Primitive Specification: Architecture governs all Primitive law; Specification governs one Primitive definition.
- State versus Lifecycle: State defines governed condition; Lifecycle defines progression semantics over time.
- Policy versus Decision: Policy defines constraints and permissions; Decision defines governed determination outcomes.
- Decision versus Event: Decision is an authoritative determination; Event is a meaningful constitutional occurrence record.
- Event versus State: Event records occurrence; State records condition and condition history.

### PART III - PRIMITIVE ADMISSION AND REJECTION

## 17. Primitive Admission Principles

Admission SHALL be evidence-led, conservative, and constitutionally justified. No candidate may be admitted without explicit governance evidence and independent review readiness.

## 18. Primitive Admission Criteria

Every proposed Primitive SHALL demonstrate:

- ownership of exactly one foundational concept
- constitutional significance
- semantic irreducibility
- cross-domain applicability
- implementation independence
- technology neutrality
- stable architectural boundary
- independent governability
- independent reviewability
- inability to be represented adequately through existing Primitives

| Criterion | Constitutional Purpose | Mandatory or Conditional | Required Evidence | Validation Authority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Singular concept ownership | Preserve atomic semantic ownership | Mandatory | Boundary declaration and overlap test | Independent architectural review |

## 19. Primitive Admission Evidence

Required evidence SHALL include discovery traceability, boundary declarations, dependency declarations, conflict analysis, and review-ready constitutional rationale.

## 20. Primitive Rejection Criteria

A candidate SHALL be rejected when it:

- duplicates an existing Primitive
- can be composed entirely from existing Primitives
- represents an implementation concern
- represents a vendor or technology concern
- lacks cross-domain applicability
- lacks constitutional significance
- has unstable or ambiguous boundaries
- creates unavoidable circular dependencies
- cannot be independently governed
- merges multiple foundational concepts

Primitive Rejection Matrix:

| Rejection Condition | Architectural Risk | Required Finding | Candidate Disposition |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Duplicates existing Primitive | Redundant governance and semantic confusion | Existing Primitive fully covers meaning | REJECTED |
| Fully composable from existing Primitives | Primitive inflation | No irreducible concept remains | REJECTED |
| Implementation concern | Constitutional layer contamination | Candidate depends on runtime/technology specifics | REJECTED |
| Vendor or technology concern | Non-portable constitutional law | Candidate bound to a product/technology | REJECTED |
| Lacks cross-domain applicability | Narrow, non-foundational scope | Evidence limited to one local use-case | DEFERRED or REJECTED |
| Lacks constitutional significance | Weak governance relevance | No authority-level impact | REJECTED |
| Unstable or ambiguous boundaries | Conflict and drift | Boundary tests fail | REQUIRES FURTHER DISCOVERY |
| Unavoidable circular dependencies | Non-deterministic interpretation | Dependency cycle cannot be eliminated | REJECTED |
| Cannot be independently governed | Lifecycle failure risk | Candidate cannot complete governance stages | REJECTED |
| Merges multiple foundational concepts | Atomicity violation | More than one concept boundary needed | REJECTED |

## 21. Primitive Inflation Control

Genesis SHALL NOT admit a new Primitive where existing Primitives or governed composition is sufficient. Inflation control SHALL be applied at discovery, authoring, and independent review.

### PART IV - PRIMITIVE SEMANTIC BOUNDARIES

## 23. Primitive Ownership

Each Primitive SHALL own exactly one foundational semantic boundary and SHALL define the constitutional question it answers.

## 24. Primitive Exclusions

Each Primitive SHALL explicitly exclude semantics owned by other Primitives and SHALL NOT absorb external ownership through implied interpretation.

## 25. Primitive Boundary Declaration

Every Primitive specification SHALL declare:

- concept owned
- constitutional question answered
- explicit exclusions
- dependencies
- specializations
- extensions
- prohibited redefinitions
- governance authority
- unresolved boundary questions

Primitive Boundary Declaration Matrix:

| Declaration Element | Purpose | Required Content | Validation Method |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Concept owned | Define semantic ownership | One foundational concept | Boundary consistency review |
| Constitutional question answered | Define architectural necessity | Canonical constitutional question | Question-to-ownership traceability |
| Explicit exclusions | Prevent overlap | Out-of-bound semantics | Conflict simulation |
| Dependencies | Declare constitutional reliance | Directed dependency list | Dependency analysis |
| Specializations | Bound lawful narrowing | Specialization scope and preserved parent meaning | Specialization law conformance check |
| Extensions | Bound lawful additive semantics | Extension scope and compatibility constraints | Extension compatibility check |
| Prohibited redefinitions | Prevent semantic takeover | Redefinition prohibitions | Cross-primitive semantic audit |
| Governance authority | Anchor constitutional control | Governing authority references | Authority traceability check |
| Unresolved boundary questions | Preserve known uncertainty | Open items with disposition | Review issue tracking |

## 26. Boundary Immutability

Approved boundaries MAY evolve only through formal constitutional governance, independent review, and preserved lineage.

## 27. Boundary Conflict

A boundary conflict exists when two Primitives claim overlapping ownership or when one Primitive contradicts another's owned concept.

## 28. Boundary Conflict Resolution

Boundary conflicts SHALL be resolved by this order:

1. constitutional authority
2. governing architecture
3. approved Primitive lineage
4. semantic ownership
5. dependency direction
6. independent architectural review

### PART V - PRIMITIVE RELATIONSHIP MODEL

## 29. Primitive Relationship Types

Relationship types governed by this architecture are dependency, specialization, extension, composition, interaction, and reference.

Primitive Relationship Type Matrix:

| Relationship Type | Meaning | Ownership Effect | Permitted Use | Prohibited Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Dependency | One Primitive relies on another Primitive concept | No ownership transfer | Constitutional reliance declaration | Implied ownership takeover |
| Specialization | Narrowing of inherited concept while preserving original law | Parent ownership preserved, child contextualized | Domain-constrained semantic refinement | Parent redefinition |
| Extension | Additive compatible semantics beyond existing boundary | Original ownership unchanged | Add context without changing core meaning | Boundary mutation of source Primitive |
| Composition | Combine multiple Primitives in a higher-order model | No automatic new Primitive ownership | Build governed higher-order architectures | Auto-classifying composition as new Primitive |
| Interaction | Cooperative interpretation across Primitive boundaries | Ownership remains independent | Controlled cross-primitive behavior interpretation | Semantic merge |
| Reference | Citation of Primitive semantics without reliance | No ownership transfer | Traceability and documentation | Implicit dependency claims |

Required distinctions:

- Primitive dependency versus Primitive specialization
- Primitive specialization versus Primitive extension
- Primitive specialization versus Primitive redefinition
- Primitive composition versus Primitive admission
- Relationship versus Dependency
- constitutional dependency versus runtime dependency

## 30. Primitive Dependency

Dependency means requiring another Primitive's constitutional concept without redefining or owning it.

## 31. Primitive Specialization

Specialization means narrowing or applying an inherited constitutional concept while preserving its original meaning and laws.

## 32. Primitive Extension

Extension means adding compatible constitutional semantics beyond an existing Primitive boundary without modifying the original boundary.

## 33. Primitive Composition

Composition means combining multiple Primitives into a higher-order architectural model without converting the composition into a new Primitive automatically.

## 34. Primitive Interaction

Interaction means cooperation between Primitives while each retains independent semantic ownership.

## 35. Primitive Redefinition Prohibition

A Genesis Constitutional Primitive SHALL NOT redefine, replace, contradict, or weaken a concept owned by another approved Primitive.

## 36. Relationship Declaration Requirement

Every Primitive specification SHALL explicitly declare each relationship it has with every Primitive upon which it relies.

### PART VI - DEPENDENCY ARCHITECTURE

## 37. Directed Dependency Model

Primitive dependencies SHALL be explicit, directed, and reviewable constitutional declarations.

## 38. Acyclicity Law

No Genesis Constitutional Primitive may introduce a circular dependency.

Primitive dependencies SHALL form a directed acyclic graph.

## 39. Dependency Direction

A Primitive MAY depend only on an approved Primitive or on a Primitive explicitly permitted as a provisional dependency by the governing architecture and independent review.

## 40. Dependency Validation

Dependency validation SHALL cover:

- direct cycles
- indirect cycles
- mutual specialization
- hidden semantic cycles
- extension cycles
- governance cycles

Primitive Dependency Validation Matrix:

| Validation | Risk Detected | Required Result | Failure Consequence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Direct cycle detection | Immediate non-acyclic dependency | No direct cycle present | Candidate blocked from admission/approval |
| Indirect cycle detection | Multi-hop cyclic interpretation | No indirect cycle present | Candidate blocked from admission/approval |
| Mutual specialization detection | Reciprocal concept distortion | No mutual specialization loop | Boundary correction required |
| Hidden semantic cycle detection | Unstated conceptual loop | No implicit cycle via exclusions/interaction | Independent re-review required |
| Extension cycle detection | Additive loop that mutates ownership | No cycle among extension chains | Candidate deferred or rejected |
| Governance cycle detection | Circular review/authority dependence | No governance dependency cycle | Governance plan correction required |

## 41. Dependency Change Control

Any dependency change SHALL trigger boundary impact analysis, cycle revalidation, and independent architectural review before approval.

## 42. Candidate Primitive Dependency Architecture

Only relationships sufficiently supported by GPD-0001 are codified here.

Approved relationships:

- State depends on Identity.
- Relationship depends on Identity.
- Lifecycle depends on State.

Candidate relationships:

- Policy may depend on Identity, State, and Relationship.
- Decision may depend on Policy and State.
- Event may depend on Identity and constitutionally governed change.
- Dependency may depend on Identity and Relationship.

Deferred relationships:

- Event depending on Decision at primitive level is deferred pending future evidence.

No candidate relationship in this section SHALL be interpreted as already approved constitutional Primitive law.

## 47. Primitive Evolution and Baseline Integration

This architecture governs:

- admission of new Primitives
- revision of approved Primitives
- supersession
- deprecation
- rejection
- compatibility preservation
- approval lineage preservation
- immutable historical records
- baseline inclusion requirements

An approved Primitive becomes governing architecture only after controlled baseline integration. Revisions and supersessions SHALL preserve historical lineage and SHALL state compatibility impact explicitly.

Future extensibility governance:

- Relationship, Policy, Decision, Event, Lifecycle, and Dependency SHALL be authored under this architecture.
- Workflow, Runtime, Enterprise models, Knowledge models, Evidence models, AI systems, agent systems, governance systems, and generation systems MAY compose Primitives as higher-order architectures.
- Higher-order composition SHALL NOT automatically create a new Primitive classification.
- Workflow and Runtime SHALL NOT be classified as foundational Primitives unless future discovery and governance evidence supports admission.
