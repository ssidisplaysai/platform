## 2. Purpose

Constitutional Services require a master architecture before additional models are authored because milestone-level artifacts must be organized by a single authoritative architectural blueprint.

Without a master blueprint, milestone creation may drift in scope, sequencing, dependencies, governance posture, and terminology. Such drift is a program-level architectural defect because Constitutional Services are responsible for preserving constitutional authority through deterministic and reviewable architectural structures.

This artifact defines the governing architecture of the Constitutional Services program. It specifies what the program shall include, how milestones relate, what dependencies are mandatory, what governance controls apply, and what measurable conditions define completion.

This artifact is architecture only. It shall remain technology neutral, implementation independent, and constitutionally aligned.

## 3. Program Scope

Constitutional Services shall define the program architecture for constitutional operational governance artifacts above frozen constitutional baseline doctrine and below implementation systems.

Constitutional Services responsibilities shall include:
- service-architecture boundaries for constitutional governance behavior
- capability architecture and ownership architecture
- operation architecture and dependency architecture
- lineage, authority, and conformance architecture
- architectural lifecycle and governance progression criteria
- architectural traceability expectations across artifacts

Constitutional Services shall be distinguished from adjacent layers as follows:
- Genesis Vision: defines enduring enterprise architectural doctrine; Constitutional Services shall apply but shall not redefine it
- Genesis Constitution: defines constitutional authority and immutable baseline meaning; Constitutional Services shall remain subordinate
- Semantic Foundation: defines canonical semantic meaning; Constitutional Services shall consume but shall not redefine it
- Constitutional Objects: defines governed object-layer architectural contracts; Constitutional Services shall coordinate with but shall not absorb object ownership
- Enterprise Models: define business and engineering model structures; Constitutional Services shall provide governance context, not model ownership
- Compiler: defines transformation architecture and realization mechanics; Constitutional Services shall not define compiler behavior
- Runtime: defines execution architecture; Constitutional Services shall not define runtime behavior
- Implementation Specifications: define technologies, interfaces, and realization details; Constitutional Services shall not define implementation

Program limits:
- Constitutional Services shall not define runtime behavior.
- Constitutional Services shall not define compiler algorithms.
- Constitutional Services shall not define implementation contracts.
- Constitutional Services shall not define APIs, persistence, deployment, or infrastructure.
