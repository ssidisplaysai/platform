# Research Agenda

Status: Approved
Classification: Genesis Research Standard

## Purpose

This agenda defines the initial research areas for Genesis and aligns them with standards evolution and compiler-stage validation.

## RA-0001 Enterprise Semantics

- Core Question: What universal semantic primitives are sufficient to model enterprise reality across domains?
- Purpose: Validate whether semantic minimalism can remain expressive under enterprise complexity.
- Active Hypotheses:
- The five-seed semantic baseline can derive most enterprise concepts without loss of meaning.
- Semantic stability depends on strict derivation and lineage constraints.
- Example Experiments:
- customer, supplier, and contract derivation comparisons from seed primitives
- cross-industry mapping of seed-to-derived concepts
- Relationship to Standards:
- informs GBS-SEED updates and semantic derivation governance
- Relationship to Compiler Stages:
- discovery to evidence mapping
- knowledge and semantic IR validation gates

## RA-0002 Enterprise Identity

- Core Question: What invariants preserve enterprise identity through structural and operational change?
- Purpose: Define identity continuity rules that survive mergers, splits, reorganization, and policy evolution.
- Active Hypotheses:
- identity continuity requires lineage plus purpose continuity, not static organizational shape
- ownership and governance transitions can preserve identity under constrained conditions
- Example Experiments:
- longitudinal identity tracing across organizational version histories
- stress testing identity continuity under rebranding and legal restructuring scenarios
- Relationship to Standards:
- informs identity semantics and governance promotion criteria
- Relationship to Compiler Stages:
- lineage propagation and identity continuity validation in compiled artifacts

## RA-0003 Enterprise Evolution

- Core Question: How do enterprise capabilities, workflows, policies, and knowledge evolve while preserving auditable continuity?
- Purpose: Build evidence-backed models for enterprise change and maturity.
- Active Hypotheses:
- enterprise evolution can be represented as additive graph transformations with preserved lineage
- policy and workflow evolution follows recurring transition patterns that are discoverable
- Example Experiments:
- capability emergence analysis from event and outcome history
- policy drift and convergence studies with evidence-backed timelines
- Relationship to Standards:
- informs evolution semantics, lifecycle constraints, and governance rules
- Relationship to Compiler Stages:
- transformation step modeling, temporal validation, and history-preserving compilation

## RA-0004 Enterprise Compilation

- Core Question: Under what conditions can enterprise reality be deterministically compiled into evidence-backed systems?
- Purpose: Define minimum verified inputs, proof obligations, and validation criteria for trusted compilation.
- Active Hypotheses:
- deterministic compilation is achievable with strict evidence and lineage constraints
- generation quality is bounded by verification completeness prior to compilation
- Example Experiments:
- minimum input threshold experiments for deterministic knowledge and semantic IR
- reproducibility trials for repeated compilation under controlled evidence sets
- Relationship to Standards:
- informs compiler-facing governance and promotion thresholds
- Relationship to Compiler Stages:
- evidence IR integrity, knowledge IR determinism, semantic validation, and genome validation criteria
