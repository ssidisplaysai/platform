# Peer Review Process

Status: Approved
Classification: Genesis Research Process

## Review Purpose

Peer review validates that research methods, evidence, and conclusions are rigorous enough to support discovery promotion.
It is a quality control gate, not a formality.

## Reviewer Expectations

Reviewers must assess:
- hypothesis falsifiability
- evidence quality and lineage
- method repeatability
- interpretation validity and alternative explanations
- stated limitations and unresolved risks

## Approval Criteria

A review is approved when:
- methodology is reproducible
- evidence meets quality requirements
- conclusions follow from results
- unresolved issues are non-blocking and documented

## Rejection Criteria

A review is rejected when:
- method is not reproducible
- evidence is weak or non-traceable
- conclusions overreach available evidence
- critical confounders are ignored

## Unresolved Question Handling

Unresolved questions must be recorded explicitly with:
- risk assessment
- blocking or non-blocking classification
- required follow-up experiment or evidence

Unresolved critical questions block promotion.

## Relationship to Standards Promotion

Peer review approval is necessary but not sufficient for standards promotion.
Promotion also requires governance approval, compatibility assessment, and validation against constitutional and semantic constraints.
