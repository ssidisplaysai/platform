# RA-0002 Enterprise Identity

Status: Active
Classification: Genesis Research Area

## Purpose

Study what allows an organization to preserve identity through change and define evidence-backed identity continuity constraints.

## Core Questions

- What makes a business the same business over time?
- What changes without destroying identity?
- How are lineage, ownership, purpose, and memory preserved?
- How should Genesis represent identity across versions?

## Current Hypotheses

- H1: Identity continuity is preserved when purpose lineage and accountable governance remain traceable.
- H2: Structural and branding changes do not by themselves break identity continuity.
- H3: Identity breaks can be detected through discontinuities in lineage, authority, and obligations.

## Evidence and Experiment Focus

- longitudinal analysis of organizational transformations
- ownership and governance transition case studies
- versioned identity graph modeling with explicit continuity assertions

## Expected Outputs

- identity continuity criteria and failure indicators
- evidence minimum for asserting same-entity-over-time
- candidate compiler validation checks for identity lineage integrity

## Non-Promotion Constraint

Findings in this research area are non-canonical until promoted through governance.
No direct standards or implementation changes are authorized by this file.
