# RA-0003 Enterprise Evolution

Status: Active
Classification: Genesis Research Area

## Purpose

Study how organizations grow, adapt, reorganize, and mature while preserving auditable history and semantic continuity.

## Core Questions

- How do capabilities emerge?
- How do workflows evolve?
- How does knowledge accumulate?
- How do policies change?
- How does the Enterprise Genome preserve history?

## Current Hypotheses

- H1: Capability emergence can be modeled as lineage-preserving graph expansion.
- H2: Workflow evolution follows repeatable transition motifs detectable from event evidence.
- H3: Policy change without explicit lineage increases semantic drift and governance risk.

## Evidence and Experiment Focus

- event-sequence studies for capability and workflow formation
- policy version and impact tracing
- knowledge accumulation mapping through claims and evidence lineage

## Expected Outputs

- evolution pattern taxonomy with confidence levels
- candidate lifecycle constraints for semantic and governance standards
- genome history preservation criteria for future compiler validations

## Non-Promotion Constraint

Findings in this research area are non-canonical until promoted through governance.
No direct standards or implementation changes are authorized by this file.
