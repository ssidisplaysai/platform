# Genesis Research Framework

Status: Approved
Classification: Genesis Research Standard
Type: Foundational Research Framework

## Purpose of the Research Directory

The research directory exists to discover, test, validate, and govern principles of enterprise reality before they are promoted into standards, compiler specifications, or constitutional doctrine.

Research in Genesis is pre-canonical work.
It produces evidence-backed findings and rejects unsupported assumptions.

## Relationship to Constitution

The Constitution defines foundational doctrine.
Research does not override constitutional doctrine.
Research may propose constitutional candidates only after evidence, verification, and peer review are complete.

## Relationship to Semantics

Semantics standards define approved canonical meaning.
Research explores hypotheses, limits, and boundary conditions that may later inform semantic standards.
Research outcomes remain non-canonical until promoted through governance.

## Relationship to Compiler Implementation

Research may inform future compiler constraints, validation gates, and IR requirements.
Research does not directly change implementation behavior.
Compiler changes require separate specification and implementation governance after research promotion.

## Research vs Standards vs Documentation

Research:
- asks and tests questions
- records hypotheses, experiments, evidence, and conclusions
- may fail and remain unresolved

Standards:
- define approved stable rules and constraints
- govern canonical behavior and meaning
- require formal promotion and compatibility controls

Documentation:
- describes architecture, operations, and implementation state
- can report both research and standards outcomes
- is not itself a promotion authority

## Research Lifecycle Overview

1. Observation
2. Question
3. Hypothesis
4. Evidence Collection
5. Experiment Design
6. Prototype or Modeling
7. Verification
8. Peer Review
9. Discovery
10. Specification Candidate
11. Compiler Validation
12. Canonical Promotion
