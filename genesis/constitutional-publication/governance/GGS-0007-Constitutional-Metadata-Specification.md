# GGS-0007

Title: Constitutional Metadata Specification
Status: Active
Authority: Foundation Authority
Program: GCP-0001

## Purpose

This specification defines the constitutional meaning of metadata for every constitutional artifact within the Genesis Enterprise Semantic Computing Platform.

Metadata exists to make a constitutional artifact governable, discoverable, relatable, reviewable, publishable, and auditable without redefining the artifact's normative content.

This specification defines constitutional meaning only. It does not define serialization formats, schemas, databases, APIs, or automation.

## Constitutional Position

Metadata is constitutional context, not constitutional subject matter.

Metadata exists to preserve intelligibility of an artifact's constitutional standing, relationships, lineage, and governed history.

Metadata shall never replace the artifact's substantive content, but no artifact may be fully governable without sufficient metadata meaning.

## Metadata Categories

### Identity

Identity metadata preserves the constitutional distinctness of the artifact as a governed subject.

Its purpose is to bind metadata to the correct constitutional artifact and preserve continuity across lifecycle events.

### Classification

Classification metadata preserves the constitutional class, family, and interpretive role of the artifact.

Its purpose is to indicate what kind of governed subject the artifact is within Genesis constitutional order.

### Constitutional Authority Level

Constitutional Authority Level metadata preserves the artifact's position within the Genesis authority hierarchy.

Its purpose is to make authority boundaries and dependency legitimacy intelligible.

### Repository Ownership

Repository Ownership metadata preserves accountable stewardship for publication, correctness, review readiness, and lineage integrity.

Its purpose is to bind constitutional accountability to repository truth.

### Review History

Review History metadata preserves the governed trace of review classes, dispositions, and review continuity material to the artifact's standing.

Its purpose is to make legitimacy auditable and publication standing intelligible.

### Publication History

Publication History metadata preserves the governed record of how the artifact entered, changed standing within, or exited canonical repository publication.

Its purpose is to preserve publication lineage and historical interpretability.

### Parent Relationships

Parent relationship metadata preserves immediate constitutional structural placement.

Its purpose is to prevent orphaned constitutional subjects and preserve navigation.

### Child Relationships

Child relationship metadata preserves constitutionally subordinate or structurally contained artifacts.

Its purpose is to preserve decompositional intelligibility.

### Related Artifacts

Related artifact metadata preserves materially relevant but non-hierarchical relationships.

Its purpose is to preserve context without collapsing other relationship types.

### Dependency Relationships

Dependency metadata preserves which other governed artifacts are necessary for constitutional coherence, governance coherence, publication legitimacy, or interpretive completeness.

Its purpose is to preserve truthful dependency reasoning.

### Version Information

Version information metadata preserves the artifact's governed standing within its lineage.

Its purpose is to distinguish revision, succession, freezing, supersession, and retirement without identity confusion.

### Lifecycle State

Lifecycle State metadata preserves current constitutional standing within the governed lifecycle.

Its purpose is to make artifact status intelligible and actionable within governance.

### Keywords

Keyword metadata preserves discovery-oriented interpretive markers that support repository searchability and human navigation.

Its purpose is discovery, not classification replacement.

### Domain

Domain metadata preserves the constitutional subject area or subsystem to which the artifact belongs.

Its purpose is to support structural intelligibility across programs and repositories.

### Layer

Layer metadata preserves the artifact's position within the constitutional authority and architectural stack.

Its purpose is to prevent cross-layer ambiguity and illegitimate dependency flow.

### Provenance

Provenance metadata preserves where the artifact came from in constitutional terms, including origin of authorship, recovery standing, and publication source legitimacy.

Its purpose is to preserve trust in constitutional origin.

### Traceability

Traceability metadata preserves the artifact's navigable connections to reviews, publication history, lineage, milestone context, dependencies, and related constitutional structures.

Its purpose is to make the artifact explainable within the broader Genesis constitutional system.

## Required Metadata

Required metadata is the minimum constitutional context necessary to make an artifact governable and intelligible.

Required metadata shall exist where absence would prevent:
- identity recognition
- classification recognition
- ownership recognition
- lifecycle recognition
- review traceability
- publication intelligibility
- relationship intelligibility
- lineage intelligibility

Required metadata is determined by constitutional necessity, not implementation convenience.

## Optional Metadata

Optional metadata is additional governed context that may improve discoverability, navigation, or interpretive richness without being required for basic constitutional legitimacy.

Optional metadata shall never contradict or obscure required metadata.

Optional metadata shall remain subordinate to constitutional truth and shall not create ambiguity about artifact standing.

## Prohibited Metadata

Prohibited metadata is metadata whose presence would violate constitutional independence, governance clarity, or long-term repository integrity.

Prohibited metadata includes any metadata that:
- attempts to redefine artifact identity implicitly
- embeds implementation-specific assumptions as constitutional meaning
- collapses distinct relationship types into ambiguous descriptors
- introduces non-governed publication standing
- encodes transient execution detail as constitutional truth
- obscures provenance, lineage, or review continuity

## Metadata Immutability Rules

Metadata immutability shall be governed by constitutional meaning.

Metadata that expresses identity, lineage, authority level, structural truth, or historical review and publication truth shall not be mutable without governed change discipline.

Metadata that records historical constitutional events shall be append-preserving rather than retroactively rewritten where constitutional history would otherwise be obscured.

Metadata evolution shall preserve:
- identity continuity
- review continuity
- publication continuity
- lineage continuity
- audit intelligibility

## Constitutional Metadata Doctrine

Metadata shall remain:
- platform independent
- implementation independent
- serialization independent
- schema independent
- technology independent

Metadata meaning shall always precede machine representation.
