# GGS-0002

Title: Publication Manifest Specification
Status: Active
Authority: Foundation Authority
Program: GCP-0001

## Purpose

The Publication Manifest defines the governed conceptual meaning of the constitutional publication fields required to describe a constitutional artifact as a publishable repository subject.

This specification defines field meaning, not serialization, storage, file format, database model, or APIs.

## Scope

This specification governs the constitutional meaning of publication manifest concepts required to represent publication identity, structural placement, review traceability, and repository intelligibility.

## Constitutional Position

A publication manifest is not the constitutional artifact itself. It is the governed publication descriptor that makes the artifact intelligible within repository truth, publication lifecycle, review history, and constitutional discovery.

A manifest therefore serves publication meaning and governance clarity, not implementation convenience.

## Artifact Identity

Artifact Identity in a publication manifest means the governed constitutional identity of the published subject.

Its purpose is to declare which constitutional subject the manifest describes.

Identity shall distinguish the artifact from every other constitutional subject and shall preserve continuity across review, publication, recovery, audit, and succession.

## Version

Version means the governed publication standing of a constitutional artifact within its lineage.

Version exists to distinguish successive published states without obscuring continuity of subject.

Version shall support:
- repository intelligibility
- publication traceability
- review binding
- successor reasoning

Version shall not be treated as a substitute for substantive governance review.

## Repository Location

Repository Location means the governed repository placement at which the constitutional artifact is authoritatively discoverable.

Its purpose is to bind repository truth to constitutional publication truth.

Repository Location shall support discoverability, audit, and milestone understanding.

Repository Location shall not determine artifact legitimacy by itself.

## Parent

Parent means the immediate governing or structural artifact under which the published artifact is constitutionally situated.

Its purpose is to preserve structural intelligibility and prevent orphaned publication subjects.

Parent shall not be confused with dependency, ownership, or review authority.

## Children

Children means the artifacts constitutionally situated beneath the published artifact in governed structure.

Its purpose is to preserve decomposition visibility, milestone comprehension, and structured discovery.

Child listing shall support repository navigation and audit completeness.

## Related Artifacts

Related Artifacts means artifacts materially associated with the subject but not necessarily parent, child, or dependency.

Its purpose is to preserve constitutional context without collapsing distinct relationship types.

Related Artifacts shall remain semantically distinguishable from dependencies and lineage relationships.

## Review History

Review History means the governed record of review classes and dispositions materially relevant to the artifact's constitutional standing.

Its purpose is to answer:
- what review classes examined this artifact
- what dispositions were issued
- what review continuity binds the current published standing

Review History shall be complete enough to support publication legitimacy and repository audit.

## Publication State

Publication State means the governed standing of the artifact in the constitutional publication lifecycle.

Its purpose is to distinguish artifacts that are draft, review-ready, publication-ready, published, retired, or superseded.

Publication State shall remain distinct from review status and distinct from authoring progress.

## Dependencies

Dependencies means the governed set of artifacts whose existence or approved standing is necessary for the artifact to remain constitutionally coherent or publishable.

Its purpose is to preserve semantic and governance integrity.

Dependencies shall not be inferred from incidental citation alone.

## Keywords

Keywords means the governed discovery terms by which an artifact may be constitutionally located, understood, and related within broader repository truth.

Keywords exist for discoverability and interpretive navigation.

Keywords shall not replace classification, lineage, or formal relationships.

## Layer

Layer means the constitutional stratum in which the artifact operates within Genesis architectural, semantic, governance, or publication structure.

Its purpose is to preserve architectural intelligibility and prevent cross-layer confusion.

Layer shall support long-term discovery and governance review routing.

## Domain

Domain means the governed constitutional subject area to which the artifact belongs.

Its purpose is to preserve subsystem and subject alignment across theory, governance, publication, milestones, reviews, indexes, and future domains.

Domain shall not be used to obscure parentage or lineage.

## Owner

Owner means the accountable steward of the artifact's constitutional correctness, publication readiness, review readiness, and lineage continuity.

Owner exists to preserve accountability, not to bypass review or publication governance.

## Governance Meaning of the Manifest

A publication manifest shall make a constitutional artifact publication-intelligible.

It shall answer, at minimum:
- what this artifact is
- where it belongs
- what governs it
- what it depends on
- what reviewed it
- what its publication standing is
- how it relates to adjacent artifacts
