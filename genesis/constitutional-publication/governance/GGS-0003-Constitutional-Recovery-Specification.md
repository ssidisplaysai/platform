# GGS-0003

Title: Constitutional Recovery Specification
Status: Active
Authority: Foundation Authority
Program: GCP-0001

## Purpose

This specification defines the constitutional recovery lifecycle by which conversational or provisional constitutional material becomes governed, reviewed, canonically published repository truth.

This specification governs lifecycle meaning only. It does not define implementation systems, workflow engines, storage models, or APIs.

## Constitutional Position

Constitutional recovery is the governed transformation of provisional constitutional material into canonical constitutional artifact standing.

Recovery shall not be treated as informal transcription. It is a constitutional act of governed publication.

## Stage Meanings

### Conversation Draft

Conversation Draft means provisional constitutional material exists in conversational or pre-repository form.

Its purpose is to preserve candidate constitutional content before governed review.

Conversation Draft shall not be treated as canonical constitutional truth.

### Architecture Review

Architecture Review means the candidate material is examined for architectural coherence, subsystem integrity, scope fitness, and compatibility with Genesis constitutional foundations.

Its purpose is to determine whether the material is architecturally intelligible and governance-worthy.

### Governance Review

Governance Review means the candidate material is examined for constitutional authority alignment, lifecycle legitimacy, relationship correctness, and governance compliance.

Its purpose is to determine whether the artifact may proceed toward canonical standing under Genesis governance.

### Publication Review

Publication Review means the candidate material is examined for publication completeness, repository intelligibility, discoverability, and publication readiness.

Its purpose is to ensure the repository can serve as authoritative truth after publication.

### Canonicalization

Canonicalization means the reviewed material is formally recognized as the canonical governed constitutional subject to be published.

Its purpose is to distinguish the winning governed artifact from prior draft states, competing candidates, or conversational variants.

Canonicalization shall not occur before required reviews are completed.

### Metadata Assignment

Metadata Assignment means the canonical artifact is given its governed publication descriptors and constitutional positioning attributes.

Its purpose is to make the artifact intelligible within publication, recovery, registry, review, and audit systems.

Metadata Assignment shall follow canonicalization and shall not substitute for it.

### Registry Update

Registry Update means the constitutional registry is updated to reflect the governed existence, standing, lineage, and relationships of the canonical artifact.

Its purpose is to bind constitutional governance truth to repository truth.

### Index Update

Index Update means repository indexes are updated to preserve discovery, navigation, milestone intelligibility, and review traceability.

Its purpose is to ensure a new engineer or auditor can find and understand the artifact within its governed context.

### Repository Publication

Repository Publication means the canonical artifact is placed into authoritative repository standing under governed structure.

Its purpose is to make the artifact the discoverable published constitutional truth of record.

### Validation

Validation means the published artifact, its registry standing, and its index presence are checked for constitutional consistency, publication integrity, and repository completeness.

Its purpose is to confirm publication truth is structurally and constitutionally sound.

### Published Artifact

Published Artifact means the artifact has completed the governed recovery lifecycle and now exists as canonical repository truth.

Its purpose is to mark the completion of recovery and the beginning of normal governed constitutional lifecycle management.

## Recovery Obligations

Constitutional recovery shall:
- preserve provenance from draft to publication
- preserve review class traceability
- preserve canonicalization rationale
- preserve registry and index consistency
- preserve repository intelligibility

Constitutional recovery shall not:
- bypass review classes
- publish draft material as canonical truth
- separate publication from registry truth
- omit validation before publication standing is recognized
