# GGS-0009

Title: Constitutional Authority Specification
Status: Active
Authority: Foundation Authority
Program: GCP-0001

## Purpose

This specification defines the constitutional authority hierarchy of the Genesis Enterprise Semantic Computing Platform.

Its purpose is to establish how higher constitutional meaning governs lower authority, how boundaries are preserved, how dependencies remain legitimate, and how escalation must occur when lower authority conflicts with higher authority.

This specification defines constitutional meaning only. It does not define implementation systems, automation, storage, APIs, or serialization.

## Constitutional Doctrine

Higher constitutional authority governs lower authority. Lower authority shall never redefine higher authority.

This doctrine is permanent and binding across all future constitutional artifacts, standards, specifications, automation, implementations, and publication systems.

## Authority Levels

### Level 0 - Enterprise Semantic Computing Theory

Purpose:
Define the highest-order conceptual truths and first-order semantic foundations.

Responsibilities:
- define foundational meaning
- define conceptual boundaries
- anchor lower-order doctrine

Ownership:
Foundation Authority and constitutional theory stewardship.

Scope:
Theory-level constitutional truth.

Authority Boundaries:
May govern all lower levels. May not be redefined by lower levels.

Permitted Dependencies:
None lower than itself.

Forbidden Dependencies:
No dependence on lower authority for foundational legitimacy.

Override Rules:
Overrides all lower authority when conflict exists.

Escalation Rules:
Conflicts at this level require highest constitutional adjudication.

### Level 1 - Constitutional Principles

Purpose:
Define enduring normative truths derived from theory.

Responsibilities:
- state permanent principles
- constrain lower-order governance and standards

Ownership:
Foundation Authority.

Scope:
Normative constitutional principles.

Authority Boundaries:
Governed by Level 0 only. Governs Levels 2 through 7.

Permitted Dependencies:
Level 0.

Forbidden Dependencies:
Levels 2 through 7.

Override Rules:
Overrides all lower levels except Level 0.

Escalation Rules:
Conflicts escalate to Level 0 or constitutional governance.

### Level 2 - Constitutional Standards

Purpose:
Define binding standards derived from principles and theory.

Responsibilities:
- state mandatory normative standards
- stabilize reusable constitutional obligations

Ownership:
Standards Authority.

Scope:
Cross-subsystem constitutional standards.

Authority Boundaries:
Governed by Levels 0 and 1. Governs Levels 3 through 7.

Permitted Dependencies:
Levels 0 and 1.

Forbidden Dependencies:
Levels 3 through 7 as foundational justification.

Override Rules:
Overrides lower levels except where higher levels govern otherwise.

Escalation Rules:
Conflicts escalate to principles or theory review as needed.

### Level 3 - Governance Specifications

Purpose:
Define how constitutional artifacts are reviewed, published, related, validated, audited, and governed.

Responsibilities:
- define governance semantics
- define lifecycle semantics
- define publication legitimacy semantics

Ownership:
Governance Authority.

Scope:
Governance and constitutional control systems.

Authority Boundaries:
Governed by Levels 0 through 2. Governs Levels 4 through 7 where governance meaning is relevant.

Permitted Dependencies:
Levels 0 through 2.

Forbidden Dependencies:
Levels 4 through 7 as sources of constitutional governance meaning.

Override Rules:
Overrides lower levels on governance standing.

Escalation Rules:
Conflicts escalate to standards, principles, or theory based on violated authority.

### Level 4 - Architecture Specifications

Purpose:
Define subsystem and platform architecture under higher authority constraints.

Responsibilities:
- define architecture responsibilities
- define subsystem boundaries
- define integration principles

Ownership:
Architecture Authority.

Scope:
Platform and subsystem architecture.

Authority Boundaries:
Governed by Levels 0 through 3. Governs Levels 5 through 7.

Permitted Dependencies:
Levels 0 through 3.

Forbidden Dependencies:
Levels 5 through 7 as architectural authority sources.

Override Rules:
Overrides engineering, runtime, and implementation when architecture conflict exists.

Escalation Rules:
Conflicts escalate to governance or standards review when constitutional meaning is implicated.

### Level 5 - Engineering Specifications

Purpose:
Define engineering realization requirements beneath architecture.

Responsibilities:
- define engineering obligations
- define validation and certification expectations
- define package-level or release-level readiness meaning

Ownership:
Engineering Governance.

Scope:
Engineering-level realization rules.

Authority Boundaries:
Governed by Levels 0 through 4. Governs Levels 6 and 7 where engineering meaning is relevant.

Permitted Dependencies:
Levels 0 through 4.

Forbidden Dependencies:
Level 7 as source of engineering legitimacy.

Override Rules:
Overrides runtime and implementation where engineering governance applies.

Escalation Rules:
Conflicts escalate to architecture or governance review.

### Level 6 - Runtime Specifications

Purpose:
Define runtime behavior, ownership, and execution semantics beneath architecture and engineering rules.

Responsibilities:
- define runtime semantics
- define execution ownership and subsystem interaction
- preserve alignment with higher authority

Ownership:
Runtime Architecture and Engineering.

Scope:
Runtime-layer specifications.

Authority Boundaries:
Governed by Levels 0 through 5. Governs Level 7 implementations.

Permitted Dependencies:
Levels 0 through 5.

Forbidden Dependencies:
Level 7 as source of runtime constitutional meaning.

Override Rules:
Overrides implementation when implementation conflicts with runtime specification.

Escalation Rules:
Conflicts escalate to engineering or architecture review.

### Level 7 - Implementation

Purpose:
Realize governed specifications in executable or operational form.

Responsibilities:
- implement governed behavior
- preserve fidelity to all higher authority
- avoid redefining constitutional meaning

Ownership:
Implementation teams under governance.

Scope:
Code, systems, execution mechanisms, tooling.

Authority Boundaries:
Governed by all higher levels. Governs no higher level.

Permitted Dependencies:
Levels 0 through 6 as governing authority.

Forbidden Dependencies:
None downward, because no lower level exists. Forbidden from redefining any higher level.

Override Rules:
No override authority over any higher level.

Escalation Rules:
All constitutional conflict escalates upward.

## Authority Invariants

The authority hierarchy shall preserve the following invariants:
- higher authority governs lower authority
- lower authority shall never redefine higher authority
- dependency legitimacy is bounded by authority order
- escalation is mandatory where lower authority conflicts with higher authority
- no implementation convenience may suspend constitutional hierarchy
