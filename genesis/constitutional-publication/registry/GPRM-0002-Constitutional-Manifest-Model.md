# GPRM-0002

Title: Constitutional Manifest Model
Status: Active
Authority: Foundation Authority
Program: GCP-0001

**This specification implements constitutional governance defined by GGS-0001 through GGS-0010 and introduces no new constitutional meaning.**

## Manifest Purpose

The Constitutional Manifest Model defines the mechanical publication descriptor structure by which a constitutional artifact is represented as a publishable repository subject.

Its purpose is to operationalize the constitutional manifest meaning defined by GGS-0002 while remaining subordinate to constitutional identity, metadata, lifecycle, authority, and dependency doctrine.

## Manifest Ownership

The manifest model is owned by constitutional publication governance implementation under Foundation Authority.

Ownership means stewardship of faithful publication mechanics, not the authority to redefine constitutional publication meaning.

## Manifest Lifecycle

The manifest model shall exist in governed relation to the lifecycle of the artifact it describes.

Manifest lifecycle shall preserve the truth that the manifest describes publication standing; it shall not independently create publication legitimacy.

Manifest evolution shall follow the constitutional lifecycle and publication mechanics rather than invent parallel status doctrine.

## Manifest Relationship to Registry

The manifest model is publication-facing and artifact-descriptive, while the registry model is governance-facing and system-compositional.

The manifest and registry shall remain mutually consistent, but neither shall collapse the role of the other.

The manifest shall support publication intelligibility. The registry shall support constitutional standing intelligibility.

## Manifest Consistency

Manifest consistency means the manifest faithfully reflects the artifact's governed identity, relationships, lineage, review standing, publication standing, and repository location as constitutionally defined elsewhere.

Manifest consistency shall fail whenever the manifest diverges materially from registry truth, artifact truth, review truth, or publication truth.

## Manifest Invariants

- Manifest meaning is descriptive of governed publication standing and does not create new constitutional meaning.
- Manifest lineage cannot diverge from artifact lineage.
- Manifest identity cannot diverge from artifact identity.
- Manifest review and publication standing cannot contradict governance truth.
- Manifest mechanics shall never redefine metadata meaning.
- Manifest consistency shall be preserved relative to registry and index truth.

## Manifest Evolution

Manifest evolution means the mechanical publication descriptor may change only as the artifact's governed standing, relationships, lineage, or publication state change.

Manifest evolution shall preserve:
- identity continuity
- lineage continuity
- review continuity
- publication continuity
- repository intelligibility

Manifest evolution shall not obscure prior publication standing or silently redefine the artifact's constitutional role.
