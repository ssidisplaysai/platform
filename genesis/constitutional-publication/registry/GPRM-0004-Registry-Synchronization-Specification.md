# GPRM-0004

Title: Registry Synchronization Specification
Status: Active
Authority: Foundation Authority
Program: GCP-0001

**This specification implements constitutional governance defined by GGS-0001 through GGS-0010 and introduces no new constitutional meaning.**

## Purpose

This specification defines the synchronization doctrine by which repository, registry, manifest, indexes, reviews, and publications remain mechanically aligned under the constitutional governance model.

Its purpose is to implement consistency obligations without redefining any constitutional identity, lifecycle, authority, dependency, review, or publication meaning.

## Synchronization Doctrine

Synchronization means that all mechanical truth surfaces representing constitutional standing remain materially aligned and mutually intelligible.

Synchronization doctrine exists to prevent drift, ambiguity, contradictory standing, and publication incoherence.

## Synchronization Domains

Synchronization shall be maintained among:
- Repository
- Registry
- Manifest
- Indexes
- Reviews
- Publications

## Synchronization Invariants

- Registry truth equals repository truth for governed standing.
- Manifest truth equals artifact publication standing.
- Indexes reflect but do not redefine registry and manifest truth.
- Reviews remain the authoritative basis for review standing.
- Publication standing shall not contradict review standing.
- No synchronization process may invent new constitutional meaning.

## Consistency Rules

Consistency rules shall require that:
- identity remains continuous across all surfaces
- lineage remains consistent across all surfaces
- lifecycle standing remains consistent across all surfaces
- dependency standing remains consistent across all surfaces
- review history remains consistent with approved review outcomes
- publication standing remains consistent with actual governed publication state

## Recovery Behavior

Where drift or inconsistency is discovered, recovery behavior shall restore alignment according to governing constitutional truth rather than convenience.

Recovery behavior shall preserve:
- auditability
- historical intelligibility
- identity continuity
- lineage continuity

Recovery behavior shall not silently rewrite constitutional standing.

## Drift Detection

Drift detection means identifying material divergence between registry, manifest, indexes, reviews, publications, and repository reality.

Drift shall be understood as a mechanical integrity problem, not a license to reinterpret governance meaning.

## Reconciliation Principles

Reconciliation means restoring synchronized truth according to the governing constitutional source for the disputed standing.

Reconciliation shall:
- defer to governance-defined meaning
- preserve higher-authority truth
- preserve auditability
- preserve lineage intelligibility
- preserve publication legitimacy

## Constitutional Invariants

- Synchronization never creates constitutional meaning.
- Drift detection never creates new governance rules.
- Reconciliation restores truth; it does not reinterpret truth.
- Mechanical alignment must preserve identity, lineage, lifecycle, and dependency fidelity.
- Repository, registry, manifest, index, review, and publication surfaces shall remain mutually intelligible.
