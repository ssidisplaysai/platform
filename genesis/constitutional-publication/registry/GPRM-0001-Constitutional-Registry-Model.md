# GPRM-0001

Title: Constitutional Registry Model
Status: Active
Authority: Foundation Authority
Program: GCP-0001

**This specification implements constitutional governance defined by GGS-0001 through GGS-0010 and introduces no new constitutional meaning.**

## Registry Purpose

The Constitutional Registry Model defines the mechanical structure by which governed constitutional artifacts are represented as a coherent registry subject within the repository.

Its purpose is to faithfully operationalize the registry meaning established by GGS-0001 without redefining identity, lineage, authority, lifecycle, dependency, review, or publication semantics.

## Registry Responsibilities

The registry model shall:
- represent every governed constitutional artifact as a registry subject
- preserve artifact identity continuity
- preserve classification, lineage, and relationship intelligibility
- preserve publication and review standing intelligibility
- support repository discoverability and audit
- support synchronization with manifests, indexes, reviews, and publication state

## Registry Ownership

The registry model is owned by constitutional publication governance implementation under Foundation Authority.

Ownership of the model means stewardship of faithful implementation of governance semantics. It does not confer authority to redefine those semantics.

## Registry Scope

The registry model governs:
- registry composition
- registry partitioning
- registry consistency obligations
- registry synchronization obligations
- discoverability mechanics
- integrity mechanics

The registry model does not govern:
- constitutional identity doctrine
- constitutional metadata meaning
- authority hierarchy meaning
- lifecycle meaning
- dependency doctrine

Those meanings remain governed exclusively by GGS-0001 through GGS-0010.

## Registry Composition

The registry shall be mechanically comprehensible as a structured constitutional representation of governed artifacts and their standing.

Composition shall preserve at minimum the mechanical representation of:
- artifact presence
- artifact identity continuity
- artifact classification
- lifecycle standing
- review standing
- publication standing
- lineage standing
- relationship standing
- dependency standing

## Registry Partitions

The registry model may distinguish internal mechanical partitions where such partitions improve clarity, discoverability, auditability, or synchronization.

Partitions exist to organize registry truth, not to divide constitutional meaning.

All partitions shall remain subordinate to one coherent registry truth surface.

## Registry Consistency

Registry consistency means the registry model faithfully agrees with governed repository truth, manifest truth, index truth, review truth, and publication truth.

Consistency shall require that no artifact's constitutional standing becomes mechanically ambiguous between these surfaces.

## Registry Synchronization

Registry synchronization means the registry model remains aligned with the current governed standing of artifacts, manifests, indexes, reviews, and publication outcomes.

Synchronization shall preserve constitutional fidelity and shall not independently invent artifact standing.

## Registry Discoverability

Registry discoverability means a competent engineer or governor can locate and understand the governed standing of a constitutional artifact through the registry model.

The registry model shall support human and future automation discoverability without redefining constitutional identity or relationships.

## Registry Integrity

Registry integrity means the registry model preserves truthful identity continuity, truthful relationship representation, truthful lifecycle standing, and truthful publication standing.

Integrity shall fail whenever the registry model obscures lineage, contradicts review or publication truth, or permits constitutional ambiguity.

## Registry Invariants

- Registry truth equals repository truth for governed artifact standing.
- Registry entries always preserve artifact identity.
- Registry mechanics shall never redefine constitutional meaning.
- Registry lineage shall remain consistent with artifact lineage.
- Registry relationships shall remain consistent with governance-defined relationship semantics.
- Registry standing shall remain synchronized with review and publication standing.
- Registry partitions shall not fragment constitutional truth.
