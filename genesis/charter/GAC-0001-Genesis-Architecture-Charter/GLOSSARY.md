# Glossary

## Reality

The enterprise as it exists in the world, including people, constraints, behavior, and context.

## Discovery

The disciplined process of revealing what is true, relevant, and consequential.

## Evidence

Verifiable observations that support, challenge, or refine understanding.

## Observation

Ongoing attention to outcomes, signals, and behavior across operations.

## Fact

A specific statement about reality that can be validated through evidence.

## Knowledge

Canonical, organized understanding derived from evidence and context.

## Business Genome

The enduring structural identity of an enterprise, including capabilities, relationships, and constraints.

## Canonical Blueprint

The coherent architectural representation of enterprise knowledge prepared for engineering.

## Enterprise

A purposeful system of people, responsibilities, capabilities, and outcomes.

## Enterprise Engineering

The discipline of faithfully translating enterprise knowledge into governed realizations.

## Knowledge Platform

The platform responsible for discovery, evidence, and canonical understanding.

## Engineering Platform

The platform responsible for architecture, capability composition, and governed transformation.

## Execution Platform

The platform responsible for operational realization and observed behavior.

## Capability

A stable unit of intended enterprise ability independent of specific implementation.

## Artifact

A persistent engineering expression of knowledge, design, validation, or operation.

## Engineering

The governed transformation of enterprise knowledge into deterministic, traceable outcomes.

## Execution

The operation of realized systems within enterprise context.

## Platform

A coordinated environment that exposes reusable workflows and capabilities for a purpose.

## Kernel

The coordinating core that maintains consistency of engineering transformations.

## Registry

A governed discovery mechanism for identities, relationships, contracts, and reusable assets.

## Workflow

A composed sequence of capabilities that advances work through defined states.

## Module

An implementation unit that realizes one or more capabilities.

## Generator

A mechanism that produces governed artifacts from defined knowledge and engineering inputs.

## Pipeline

A controlled flow of transformations from engineered intent to realized outcomes.

## Traceability

The ability to follow lineage from source understanding to resulting artifact and behavior.

## Determinism

The property that equivalent inputs and conditions produce equivalent outcomes.

## Governance

The accountable control of decisions, constraints, and change across the lifecycle.

## Identity

The intrinsic meaning and continuity of an enterprise element independent of representation.

## Representation

A model or artifact that expresses an identity in a usable form.

## Lineage

The chain of derivation connecting artifacts and decisions over time.

## Provenance

The recorded origin, context, and custodianship of knowledge and artifacts.
