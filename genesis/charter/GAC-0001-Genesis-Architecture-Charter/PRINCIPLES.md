# Principles

## 1. Reality before Software

Meaning: Enterprise truth originates in lived reality, not in tools or code.
Architectural consequence: Architecture begins with discovery of real conditions before any implementation decision.

## 2. Knowledge before Implementation

Meaning: Implementation follows canonical understanding.
Architectural consequence: Knowledge artifacts precede realization artifacts.

## 3. Engineering before Execution

Meaning: Operation should be guided by engineered intent.
Architectural consequence: Executable behavior derives from governed engineering models.

## 4. Evidence before Approval

Meaning: Decisions require observable support.
Architectural consequence: Validation and traceability are required before advancement.

## 5. Governance before Deployment

Meaning: Change must be accountable before it is operationalized.
Architectural consequence: Governance checks are integrated into lifecycle transitions.

## 6. Determinism before Convenience

Meaning: Repeatability is favored over ad hoc speed.
Architectural consequence: Transformations prioritize predictable outcomes from known inputs.

## 7. Capabilities before Implementations

Meaning: The enterprise is designed around stable capability intent.
Architectural consequence: Implementations are treated as replaceable realizations of capabilities.

## 8. Platforms before Products

Meaning: Durable platform structure precedes product expression.
Architectural consequence: Shared engineering foundations are established before specialized applications.

## 9. Identity before Representation

Meaning: What a thing is comes before how it is modeled.
Architectural consequence: Identity and purpose are defined before structural encoding.

## 10. Relationships before Hierarchies

Meaning: Connected meaning is primary; structure is secondary.
Architectural consequence: Relationship integrity is preserved across models and artifacts.

## 11. Composition before Duplication

Meaning: Reuse through composition is preferred over repetitive construction.
Architectural consequence: Architecture favors modular combination of capabilities and workflows.

## 12. Evolution before Replacement

Meaning: Continuity of knowledge is preserved through change.
Architectural consequence: Transformations extend lineage instead of discarding prior understanding.

## 13. Observation before Assumption

Meaning: Inference follows measured reality.
Architectural consequence: Operational feedback informs refinement of knowledge and engineering.

## 14. Simplicity before Complexity

Meaning: Clarity is preferred to accidental intricacy.
Architectural consequence: Designs reduce unnecessary coupling and preserve comprehensibility.

## 15. Human Judgment before Automation

Meaning: Purpose and accountability remain human responsibilities.
Architectural consequence: Automation is bounded as capability support, not governing authority.

## 16. Conservation of Understanding

Meaning: Transformations preserve or increase enterprise understanding.
Architectural consequence: Lineage, provenance, and traceability are maintained across the lifecycle.
